A strong portfolio is not just a gallery of screenshots. It should communicate how you think, what you can build, and how you present technical work professionally.

## What matters most

- Clear positioning: your name, role, and goals should be obvious immediately.
- Strong project writing: explain the problem, not just the technologies.
- Good UX: responsive layout, fast loading, and thoughtful visual hierarchy.
- Credibility: real links, code repositories, and consistent design quality.

## How I approach it

I try to build portfolio sections like product features. The homepage needs a clear story, the projects need meaningful proof, and the contact flow needs to work without friction.

That means I care about performance, accessibility, responsiveness, and maintainable code structure, not only aesthetics.
